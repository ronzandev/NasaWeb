Massive Data Haul Helps Scientists Better Understand Dangerous, Complex Hurricanes

An eye that winked, a rain of ice crystals, and striking lightning show that hurricanes lead more complex lives than anyone had suspected. More details - and fuller understanding of one of nature's more ferocious weather phenomena will come in the next year as scientists analyze thousands of hours of data collected during the most comprehensive hurricane study project ever conducted. NASA scientists, working with colleagues at NOAA, universities from Wisconsin to Florida, and reaserchers in the military and the experiment. Click on the full story link below or on http://www1.msfc.nasa.gov/NEWSROOM/news/releases/1999/99010.html to see some interesting photos and video, and hear a scientist explain the experiment in her own words.
